1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business.

IT and the computers involved help to facilitate the operations of modern business by making communication (between staff, management, customers and partners), inventory of business products, storage of data, information management of staff and customers for example. Furthermore, data analysis is an increasingly important tool in modern business with successful companies employing computer statistical software and learning algorithms to best target their potential customer market and increase revenue. App creation is also a huge sector in any digital business as it is a way for customers to communicate and reach out to your business in the quickest and most convenient way. www.broadviewnet.com

1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully
- do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

Digital projects require a whole range of resources to complete successfully and it is constantly evolving and changing. Today, no business could survive without web based analysts. Computers and statistical software are vital to analyse trends in buyer traffic and web searches. A successful digital project will also require a team of people with a range of roles within a project with a team leader to organise resources and guide the direction of the project and allocate relevant personnel to the right areas. There is a whole range of roles within a modern digital project: understanding digital strategy, customer experience, user experience, business analysis, design, copywriting, SEO, development, QA, and analytics. Key to modern digital business is the internet as it provides a way to connect client and customer as well as providing a window into buyer trends around the world. Digital and data analysis is also a key tool in modern digital businesses, using statistical and visualisation software such as R, python and MATLAB. Data management tools such as SQL are also a vital resource for quickly and effectively sorting, moving and organising data.

1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software
- how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

To select the right digital software and technologies for your job requires the strategy team to consider what their desired end goals will be. If you can see what your results will look like you can choose the right tools for the job. If you will be producing digital data then statistical and graphical software would be necessary, for visualisation of data and presentability of results to a broad audience. You also need to take into account the ease of use of the software and whether it fits into the skillset of your staff and if not, will you need to train up your staff or recruit new employees? This is something that might affect spending costs on the project, and therefore profit margin. If the overhead of new software is too high for minimum improvements in efficiency then this must be considered carefully by management. The tools already available might already be perfectly adequate. Choosing the right IT technologies for the job, again, requires good planning with clear set outcomes envisaged.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects
- How does GDPR impact digital projects?

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has a huge impact on the legal running of current digital businesses. Web-based content and user data has to be legally protected by all digital companies, to avoid theft of data for example. Loss, theft or wrongful sale of data can have serious repercussions for customers digital security. Data has been a major issue in recent years with companies unlawfully sharing user data and targeting internet users with tracking cookies. This has caused web-based businesses to conduct their work much more carefully with consent required for data usage (such as the acceptance of the use of cookies to target customers). GDPR now affects the way businesses can use mailing lists. Whereas previously companies made money from buying and selling mailing lists this has been tightened up in GDPR so that the customer has the right to refuse data sharing. Consent is the key to the relationship between online businesses and customers with websites now required to ask permission or â€˜acceptanceâ€™ of websiteâ€™s data practices. Ultimately, GDPR regulates two areas of digital operations: data gathering and profiling and secondly, Targeting. The law now regulates that digital companies allow a customer or user to refuse or object to data usage, in particular data sharing.
